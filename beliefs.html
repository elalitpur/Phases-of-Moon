Myths, Stories & Religious Significance

The Moon appears in folklore and religion worldwide.

Myths & Legends

Many cultures see figures in the full moon (e.g., the "Man in the Moon" or Rabbit). Goddesses like Greek Artemis, Roman Diana, or the Triple Moon symbol in pagan traditions represent lunar cycles.

Religious Beliefs

In Hinduism (common in Nepal), Purnima (full moon) is auspicious for festivals (e.g., Buddha Jayanti, Raksha Bandhan). Amavasya (new moon) is for ancestor rituals.

In Islam, the lunar calendar starts months with the crescent moon sighting (important for Ramadan and Eid).

Many traditions view full moon as a time of completion and new moon as beginnings.

Superstitions

Full moons are linked to increased madness ("lunacy"), higher birth rates, or werewolf transformations in folklore. Some avoid starting projects on waning moons or believe planting by phases affects crops.
