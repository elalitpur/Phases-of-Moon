Scientific Facts

Moon phases occur because of the changing positions of the Sun, Earth, and Moon. As the Moon orbits Earth (about every 29.5 days), we see different portions illuminated by sunlight.

The Eight Primary Phases

  • New Moon: Moon between Earth and Sun — dark side faces us.
  • Waxing Crescent: Thin sliver of light on the right.
  • First Quarter: Right half illuminated.
  • Waxing Gibbous: More than half lit, growing.
  • Full Moon: Entire face illuminated.
  • Waning Gibbous: More than half lit, shrinking.
  • Last Quarter: Left half illuminated.
  • Waning Crescent: Thin sliver on the left.
